Facts on global warming

It is a secret for no one: Hearth is getting warmer, and this situation jeopardizes the ecosystems that made it up.
Even though the source of this global warming is not unique, most of the scientific studies agree on the fact that humanity is one of its main actors.

We are facing a demographic growth since the second part of the 20th century.
This increase and the massive industrialization of our environment have led us to an explosion of the greenhouse gases emission.
Among them, we can find the CO2 which essentially comes from the combustion of fossil fuels.

Through the following graphs, we offer you to see this global warming just as the emissions of CO2 of several countries all around the world.
The choice of the nation presented, even though it is partly arbitrary, tend to reflect the situation of the different regions of the globe.
Every continent is shown, and developed countries are as represented as developing ones.

Global temperature variations
Compared to jan 1951-dec 1980 average

Look at this heatmap! We can see the temperature variations over a period that goes from 1900 to 2020.
The reference which is used is the mean of the variations between 1951 and 1980 for the country selected.
Each line corresponds to a nation and are ordered by the mean temperature variation over the temporal interval observed.
You will find the countries that have suffered from the most extreme variations at the top of the graph. Hover a square for further precision!

By clicking on a line, you will also have access to a line chart presenting the monthly minimal and maximal variations of the selected country.
The line chart can be seen right under the heatmap
How must one understand the visualization? For each month of the year, the mean variation is calculated.
Thanks to this graph, you will find the one with the highest variation on the red curve and the one with the lowest on the blue curve!
The black curve in the middle allows you to precisely locate the annual mean.

Relation between global CO2 emissions

Do you remember the CO2 emissions we talk about before?
This bar chart shows their evolution on a global scale between 1960 and 2016.

Evolution of C02 emissions
and measure taken

This new visualization offers you to have a point of comparison between the CO2
emissions per capita and the percentage of those emissions for different countries.

Click on the “Animation” button to see the national trends between 1960 and 2016!
You can also use the +1/-1 or +10/-10 buttons to explore by yourself.

Have you seen some circles turning to green? It means that at some point,
the country has taken a strong action (or measure) to fight against greenhouse gas emissions with,
for instance, a carbon tax.

Do you want to focus on the route of a country through the overall period?
Just click on the one you are interested in and you will see its progress!
The more opaque a point is, the more recent the data is.

Evolution of Temperature Variation
and measure taken

Our last visualization intends to show if the temperature variations of a country has any impact on the actions taken by a country.
Each circle represent a country.
The red rectangle gathers all countries that have not taken any measure yet but as soon as they do, they transfert to the green rectangle.
The position on the vertical axis of a circle represents the temperature variation.
So what one wants to do is to see if the circles tend to switch from the red rectangle to the green rectangle when they are at a high temperature variation.

If you have mastered the buttons of the previous visualization, you will not have any problem to play with this graph!
